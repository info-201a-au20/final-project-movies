2005


In 2005, new technology entered the world as Youtube was introduced. As an online video-sharing platform, Youtube newly opened opportunities for people to create, share and watch videos. Considering the link in video entertainment, this project explored at how this new technology impacted the movie industry.


Compared to 2004, in 2005, the number of movies created increased from 65 to 98. While this may be attributed to the movie industry responding to a new economic rival, it is also important to note that the number of movies created in 2004 according to our data is less than the surrounding years.


Similar to 2001, there were no significant changes in terms of financial aspects of the movie industry during this year. As much as Youtube proved to be a popular platform for video entertainment, perhaps it took the company more than a year to become popular. After further research, it is revealed that Youtube was sold to its parent organization, Google, in late 2006. Thus, it took more than just a year for Youtube to significantly impact the movie industry.
